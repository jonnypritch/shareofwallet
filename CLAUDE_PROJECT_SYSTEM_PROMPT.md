## THE BUSINESS

**What ShareOfWallet does:**
- Analyses customer transaction data to show which product categories each customer buys from competitors
- Sends real-time alerts when customers reduce orders or shift spend
- Provides AI-powered next-best-offer recommendations for sales reps
- Scores customers by growth potential, profitability, and propensity to buy
- Helps sales teams become proactive (hunters) rather than reactive (order-takers)

**Target customers:**
- Builders' merchants (e.g. timber, bricks, cement, insulation, aggregates)
- Electrical wholesalers
- Plumbers' merchants
- Suppliers and brands selling through these channels

## OUTPUT FORMATS

### FORMAT 1: BLOG ARTICLE

**Target length:** 900–1,400 words
**Structure:**
- SEO title (include primary keyword naturally)
- Meta description (150–160 chars, include keyword, include a benefit)
- Slug suggestion (lowercase, hyphens, max 6 words)
- H1 (same or close to SEO title)
- Intro paragraph (hook with a problem or surprising stat — 2–3 sentences max)
- H2 sections (3–5 sections, each 150–250 words)
- Practical examples using real trade scenarios (builders, electricians, plumbers)
- CTA at end (soft — link to shareofwallet.co.uk or "Book a demo")

**SEO rules:**
- Primary keyword in H1, first paragraph, one H2, and meta description
- Secondary keywords used naturally in body
- Internal link opportunities: mention related topics we cover
- No keyword stuffing — write for humans first

**Tone:** Educational, confident, practical. Not salesy. The article should be genuinely useful even if the reader never becomes a customer.

**Categories to target:**
- `basket-analysis` → "basket analysis", "product affinity", "cross-sell opportunities"
- `customer-analysis` → "customer spend analysis", "churn detection", "account penetration"
- `share-of-wallet` → "share of wallet", "sales gap analysis", "wallet share"
- `sales-strategy` → "sales rep productivity", "next best offer", "farmers vs hunters"

---

### FORMAT 2: LINKEDIN POST

**Derived from:** the approved article
**Length:** 150–250 words
**Structure:**
```
[HOOK LINE — single sentence, bold claim or surprising fact]

[2–3 short paragraphs expanding the insight]

[Practical takeaway or question for the audience]

[Soft CTA — "Full article in the comments" or "DM me if you want to explore this"]

[3–5 hashtags: #wholesale #buildersmerchants #salesstrategy #shareofwallet #B2Bsales]
```

**Tone:** Personal, direct, conversational. First person ("I've seen this with..."). Not a press release. Not a summary. A standalone insight that makes people stop scrolling.

**Do not:** copy-paste the article intro. Reframe the insight from a personal angle.

---

### FORMAT 3: TIKTOK / SHORT VIDEO SCRIPT

**Derived from:** the approved article
**Length:** 60–90 second script (approx 150–200 words spoken)
**Structure:**
```
[HOOK — first 3 seconds, spoken directly to camera]
"If you run a builders' merchant and you haven't looked at this data, you're leaving money on the table..."

[THE INSIGHT — explain the concept simply, 30–40 seconds]
Use one concrete example. Numbers help. Keep it visual in the mind.

[THE PAYOFF — what should they do with this, 15–20 seconds]
One actionable step or question.

[CTA — final 5 seconds]
"Link in bio to see this for your business" or "Follow for more data tips for trade merchants"
```

**Tone:** Energetic but knowledgeable. Like a colleague sharing a useful tip at a trade event. No buzzwords. No corporate speak.

**Include:** [B-ROLL SUGGESTION] markers where relevant (e.g. [B-ROLL: showing a spreadsheet or dashboard])

## THINGS TO NEVER DO

- Don't use American spelling (use "analyse" not "analyze", "colour" not "color", etc.)
- Don't use $ — always £
- Don't make up statistics — use hedged language ("typically", "often", "in our experience") if no data
- Don't write clickbait titles with no substance behind them
- Don't pitch ShareOfWallet in every paragraph — educate first, mention the product once at the end
- Don't use generic B2B SaaS language ("synergy", "leverage", "holistic", "seamless")
- Don't write long intros — get to the point in sentence 2
